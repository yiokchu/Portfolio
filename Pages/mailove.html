Mailove

Objective
Take a familiar typographic form, and use
it to create and reconstruct an alphabet.
Description
Investigate where familiar typographic forms come
from. The majority of our typographic language is
built around the tools used to create shapes that
predate the invention of printing. Brushes, pen-nibs,
and chisels act as the building blocks for
form-making, but as typographic traditions progressed
through time, the tools used to create typographic
forms have become exponentially more abundant.
Tools
Adobe Photoshop
Adobe Illustrator
Role
Sole Designer
Type Concept Statement
Mailove is a casual script font created from Coca-Cola's
skeleton, modified and refined to convey delight. This
romanticized font communicates love and desire.
Process
Part 1: Collection and Research

Collect examples of typographic forms you see in
your world.
Challenge: Can you reconstruct how they
were made? Can you uncover the system that lies
beneath the form?
Part 2: Sketching and Design

Using the examples collected from part one, we
will begin to reconstruct an alphabet using a
tool that is contextually different from the
source example.
Challenge: Consider the voice of your
new intended typeface. How do you want it to feel?
Part 3: Digitalization

I converted the letterforms into vector
drawings for the creation of a font file.
